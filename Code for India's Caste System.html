India's Caste System

What is India's Caste System

The Caste System is the system of dividing Hindus into castes.
There are 4 main castes and a caste which falls out of the main 4:

  • Brahims
  • Kshatriyas
  • Vaisyas
  • Sudras
  • Dalits

These four categories dictated lots of aspects in Hindu culture and social life
Brahims were recognized as priests and teachers, Kshatriyas were recognized as warriors and rulers.
Vaisyas were recognized as farmers and merchants, finally Sudras were known as labourers. This set up lots of
stereotypes, divided Hindus, the upper and lower castes always lived in segregation and their castes were always
indicated by their last names.

Why is it Happening?

The caste system dates back to almost more than a 1000 years ago, In the Manusmriti an authoritative Hindu book of
law acknowledges the caste system as the basis of order and regularity in society. But, Independent India's Constitution
banned discrimination on the basis of the caste back in 1950. They did this to bring a level playing field for everyone
and to correct historical injustices. But it is still practiced in modern day India as there is some corruption in the
government and in society.

Non Profit Group Trying to Solve the Issue

A group which is trying to fix this issue is Navasarjan. This group was founded in 1988 by
Martin Macwan a Gujarati Dalit whose mission is to eliminate discrimination based on the
caste system espically for dalits. To ensure equality regardless of caste, gender or
class. Also, to make sure the rule of law is used properly. They are active in 3,000 different
villages in India. They use their funding from different sources around the world to make
schools and safe communities for the Dalits.

3 Rights Being Violated:

Article 1:

In Article 1 all human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. But, in the caste
system Brahims and Dalits are not equal as Brahim's Get more of an advantage in life than
Dalits. For example, Brahims usually get the more high-paying jobs than the dalits due
to the lack of education the Dalits receive.

Article 23

In Article 23 everyone has the right to work, to free choice of employment. But, in the caste
system some castes are forced to do a job just based on the caste. Dalits are forced
to clean the streets or unclog sewers.

Article 16

In Article 16 men and women of full age, without any limitations due to race, nationality or religion, have the right to marry and to find a family. In the caste system, upper castes can't marry lower castes. For example, Brahims and Dalits are prohibted to marriage.

Conclusion

The Caste System in India is the system where Hindus are divided into castes. These castes determined
your job or role in society. A non-profit group trying to solve this issue is Navasarjan where they try to bring equality
regardless of gender, caste or class. Furthermore, the caste system violates many rights in the Universal Decleration of rights.
As of now the caste system is being used much less in society today as it is looked down upon.
